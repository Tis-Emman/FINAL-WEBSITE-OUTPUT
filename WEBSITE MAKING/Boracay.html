Information about Boracay

Boracay is a small island in the central Philippines. It's known for its resorts and beaches. Along the west coast, White Beach is backed by palm trees, bars and restaurants. Known as the “Island of Paradise” or “the island that never sleeps,” Boracay offers an unparalleled blend of natural wonders and entertainment.

Despite it being small, the island has no span of shortage of wondrous sights and fun-filled activities that can be enjoyed by either solo travelers, tour groups, or families. From frolicking on its many beaches, drinking and dancing until sunrise, and experiencing a variety of water sports activities, you will never run out of things to do in Boracay. Boracay is a small island in the central Philippines, approximately 315 kilometers south of Manila, covering about 10.32 square kilometers, and is renowned for its stunning white sandy beaches, particularly White Beach, which stretches 4 kilometers along the western coast. The island features a mix of flat coastal areas and hilly terrain, with its highest point being Mount Luho, offering panoramic views
